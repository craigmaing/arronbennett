### Why Lime Matters for Old Buildings

**Breathability**
Unlike cement, lime allows moisture to escape:

- Prevents damp being trapped
- Allows walls to breathe naturally
- Reduces condensation issues
- Protects stone and masonry
- Maintains building health

**Flexibility**
Lime accommodates building movement:

- Won't crack with seasonal changes
- Absorbs structural stress
- Self-healing properties
- Compatible with soft stones
- Prevents damage to historic fabric

**Authenticity**
Using appropriate materials for period buildings:

- Historically accurate restoration
- Maintains architectural integrity
- Required for listed buildings
- Conservation area compliance
- Preserves heritage

### Types of Lime We Work With

**Lime Putty**
- Traditional fat lime
- Aged for flexibility
- For internal plastering
- Finishing coats

**Natural Hydraulic Lime (NHL)**
- NHL 2 (softest, most breathable)
- NHL 3.5 (most common for repointing)
- NHL 5 (hardest, for exposed areas)
- External renders and mortars

**Hot Lime**
- Quick lime slaked on-site
- Traditional technique
- Strong, durable renders
- Historic method for important projects
